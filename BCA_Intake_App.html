🎯 Section 4

Desired Outcomes

Describe what the business looks like after the project succeeds — not what you'll build. Every outcome needs a metric, a baseline, and a target.

💡 Outcomes vs Outputs: An output is what you build ("a new checkout page"). An outcome is what changes in the business ("cart abandonment drops from 34% to 20%"). Write outcomes here.
Retail "Increase click-and-collect fulfilment rate from 72% to 95%+ within 90 days of go-live, recovering ~$1.1M/yr in currently lost orders. Measured via OMS fulfilment report, owned by Head of eCommerce."
This looks like an output (what you'll build) rather than an outcome (what changes in the business). Try: "As a result of this initiative, [metric] will change from [baseline] to [target] by [date]."
💡 Leading vs Lagging: Include at least one leading indicator (measurable within 30–60 days of go-live) and one lagging indicator (the ultimate outcome, measured over 6–12 months). This shows leadership you'll know early if it's working.
