🏢 Strategic Alignment 1 / 9
Why this matters
Your role calibrates the tone of recommendations — a CFO gets ROI focus, a CTO gets architecture detail. Urgency drives delivery sequencing and flags regulatory deadline risk in the Phase 4 ranking.
🔍 Current State 2 / 9
Why this matters
This is the most important field. Be specific — include system names, age, metrics (e.g. "68% cart abandonment"), who is affected, and how long the problem has existed. The AI extracts requirements and solution categories directly from this description.
⚠️ If you're vague here: the AI generates generic requirements that may not match your actual situation — solution recommendations will be less targeted and benefit values harder to defend.
💸 Business Impact 3 / 9
Why this matters
Include dollar amounts wherever possible. The AI uses these figures to anchor benefit values in Phase 2. Even rough estimates ("$50K–$100K risk exposure") are far more defensible than leaving it blank.
⚠️ If you leave this empty: benefit values are estimated from industry benchmarks — they'll be conservative and weaker in a board presentation than numbers drawn from your own data.
🎯 Business Goals 4 / 9
Why this matters
Write one goal per line. Use measurable targets where possible ("reduce cart abandonment from 68% to 40%"). Phase 3 traceability checks that every goal here is covered by at least one solution — unlinked goals flag as quality issues.
⚖️ Compliance Requirements 5 / 9
Why this matters
Selected compliance requirements filter and score vendors in Phase 1.6 — only vendors covering your requirements rank highly. Missing a requirement here means a non-compliant vendor could be recommended.
🛠️ Technical Stack 6 / 9
Why this matters
Your tech stack is used to score vendor integration compatibility in Phase 1.6. Accurate stack details improve vendor recommendations significantly — vendors that don't integrate with your environment are ranked down.
💰 Financial Parameters 7 / 9
Why this matters — Financial Parameters are the engine of Phase 2, 3, and 4
Budget: Solutions that exceed this are flagged as over-budget in Phase 1.5 and Phase 4. Discount rate: Directly controls every NPV calculation in Phase 2 and Phase 4 — 1% change shifts NPV by 3–8%. Industry standard is 8–12%. Time horizon: All DCF projections, payback comparisons, and IRR calculations run over this number of years. Annual revenue: Used to calibrate benefit estimates — a 1% uplift at $5M/yr = $50K; at $50M/yr = $500K.
⚠️ Discount rate matters most: The default 12% is a conservative mid-market rate. If your company has a known WACC or hurdle rate, use it — it directly changes the NPV and composite score in Phase 4.
🏗️ Constraints & Pain Points 8 / 9
Why this matters
Include alternatives already considered and rejected. This prevents the AI from recommending options you've already ruled out (e.g. "full platform rewrite rejected — $1.2M, 24 months"). The more context, the more targeted the solutions.
📊 Success Measures 9 / 9
Why this matters
KPIs from this section appear in the Phase 5 executive report and the Phase 3 traceability check. Be specific: include current baseline and target (e.g. "page load under 1s — baseline 3.2s"). Vague KPIs weaken the traceability score.

What makes a great business case

Four principles that dramatically improve your AI-generated results.

📊
Specificity
Numbers beat adjectives

Write "68% cart abandonment" not "high abandonment rate". Every metric you provide becomes a calculation anchor.

💰
Financials
Revenue unlocks accuracy

Add your annual revenue and the AI anchors all benefit values to your actual business size — not industry guesses.

🔒
Compliance
Tell us your constraints

List compliance requirements (PCI-DSS, GDPR, HIPAA). Every vendor is then screened against these — no surprises later.

🎯
Context
Pain points drive solutions

List your top 3–5 specific pain points. Each one generates at least one targeted solution.
